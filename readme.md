## Game Description

_(Briefly describe your game’s premise, setting, and goal. What is the game about? What experience should the player have?)_

The game is about Flandre, a vampire with the ability to destroy anything (specifics excluded), exploring a realm created by Patchouli, a magician, that allows her to freely use her powers instead of outside. Rules were put in place in the realm to prevent excessive damage but Flandre is capable of destroying them too, as she begins to go wild. Left with no choice, Patchouli modified the realm to make it more of a challenge.

The player is expected to navigate the realm with certain restrictions, choosing to destroyed whatever they wish, with the goal of reaching the transition to the next level.

There was a plan to add multiple small levels, more complex restrictions, and a scene that shows the storyline I had, but time constraints.

## **Game Procedure & Controls**

_(How does the player navigate the game? What are the controls? Provide a list of inputs and their functions.)_

- **A, D Keys** – Move character
- **W Key** – Jump
- **Shift** - Change Modes (Normal and Destroyer)
-  **Spacebar** - Attack (Only in Normal mode)
- **Left Mouse Point and Click** – Destroy Object (Only in Destroyer mode)

Designed to be able to operate with 1 hand on the keyboard and 1 on the mouse at all times.

## **Core Drive (Tally with Design Principles, Balance, and Intentional Design)**

_(What **motivates** the player to engage with your game? Relate this to the Octalysis Framework or other motivational principles.)_

Empowerment of Creativity & Feedback is the main motivator.

The player is free to use their abilities as they wish to complete the level. Once the player knows the controls, how they complete the level is entirely up to them. Effects and sound effects provide the player with good feedback in most of their actions.

There is also slight Development and Accomplishment in unlocking the restrictions.

## **Game Balance Efforts (Tally with Rubric)**

_(What balance considerations were made in the game? Did you focus on PvP balance, PvE balance, asymmetry, statistical balance, risk vs. reward, skill balance, or economic balance? Explain how you implemented or adjusted game balance.)_

Having the ability to destroy everything, little can be done to keep the balance other than:
- Prevent the destruction of certain objects using rules that can be destroyed.
- Making the destruction of certain objects stop further progress.
- Prevent movement in destroyer mode.

## **Unique Rule (Tally with Rubric)**

_(What unique rule did you introduce? Did you apply an elegant rule, break a conventional rule, or create asymmetry? Describe how this rule impacts gameplay and how it aligns with the rubric.)_

- The ability to destroy anything in destroyer state, including literally breaking a rule.
- Rules exist in the game visually that break most conventional rules of platformers
- Destroying important objects is certain to hinder progression.

## **Game Design Principles Incorporated**

_(Which design principles from class were intentionally applied? List at least one and explain how it enhances the player’s experience.)_

Grounding the Player: Controls are shown to the player at appropriate times but not explained to let them explore for themselves. The exception being the unique rule which requires a specific sequence of actions. I think it emphasizes the sense of player freedom.

If I could have implemented a storyline in game, there may have been player empathy.

Example:

- **Grounding the Player**: The environment provides strong visual cues to help players understand mechanics naturally.
- **Meaningful Choices**: Players must decide between fighting stronger enemies for rewards or avoiding them for safety.
- **Player Empathy**: The story and mechanics are designed to create an emotional connection with the character.
